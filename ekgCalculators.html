The Heart rate calculator uses the simple 60/RR formula to calculate heart-rate.

The QT calculator utilizes Bazett's formula to calculate a corrected QT. This formula results in a corrected QT by dividing the QT from the square root of the R-R.

There are a few different QT calculators available, Bazett's formula is often used due to its simplicity. However it should be noted that Bazett's formula is most accurate with heart rates within the range of 60-100 bpm.
