Normal sinus rhythm

RHYTHM: Regular RATE: Normal (60-100bpm)
P-WAVE: Normal (positive & precedes each QRS)
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Sinus Arrythmia

RHYTHM: Irregular, varying with respiration.
RATE: Normal (60-100bpm) & rate may increase during inspiration.
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: Heart rate frequently increases with inspiration, decreasing with expiration.



Sinus Arrest

RHYTHM: Irregular, do to pause.
RATE: Normal to slow.
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: Pause time is not an interger multiple of the P-P interval.



Sinus Bradycardia

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Slow (<60 bpm)
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Sinus Tachycardia

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Fast (>100 bpm)
P-WAVE: Normal, may merge with T-wave at very fast rates.
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: QT interval shortens with increased heart rate



Premature Atrial Complex

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE: Usually normal but depends on underlying rhythm.
P-WAVE: P-Wave morphology changes.
PR-INTERVAL: Normal or longer
QRS: 0.10 sec or less
NOTES:



Premature Ventricular Complex

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10sec), bizarre appearence
NOTES: Two PVCs together are termed a couplet while three PVCs in a row is ventricular tachycardia.



Premature Junctional Complex

RHYTHM Irregular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Present before, during (hidden) or afteer QRS, if visible it is inverted.
PR-INTERVAL: Absent or short
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Ventricular Bigeminy

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10sec), bizarre appearence
NOTES:PVC appears every second beat. (Bigeminy can be Atrial, or junctional also!)



Ventricular Trigeminy

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10sec), bizarre appearence
NOTES:PVC appears every third beat. (Bigeminy can be Atrial, or junctional also!)



Bundle Branch Block

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Normal
P-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Wide (>0.12sec), bizarre appearence
NOTES: QRS complex wide, (Described as Bunny ears, or notched)



Sinoatrial Block (Sinus Pause)

RHYTHM: Irregular when SA block occurs
RATE: Normal or slow
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12-0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: Pause time is an interger multiple of the P-P interval.



Junctional Escape Rhythm

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Slow (40-60 bpm)
P-WAVE: Present before, during (hidden) or after QRS, if visible it is inverted.
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: Can Be also a singular beat, known as Junctional Escape beat.



Accelerated Junctional Rhythm

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Normal (60-100 bpm)
P-WAVE: Present before, during (hidden) or after QRS, if visible it is inverted.
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Junctional Tachycardia

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Fast (100-180 bpm)
P-WAVE: Present before, during (hidden) or after QRS, if visible it is inverted.
PR-INTERVAL: Absent or short
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Fast (150-250 bpm)
P-WAVE: Merged with T-wave.
PR-INTERVAL: Normal (0.12 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.10 sec)
NOTES:PR-Interval can be difficult to measure.



Accelerated Idioventricular Rhythm

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: 50-120 bpm
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10 sec), bizarre looking
NOTES:



Idioventricular Rhythm

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Slow (20-40 bpm)
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10 sec), bizarre looking
NOTES:



Multifocal Atrial Tachycardia (MAT)

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE: Fast (>100 bpm)
P-WAVE: Often changing shape & size from beat to beat (at least three different forms)
PR-INTERVAL: Variable
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:T-wave is often distorted.



Atrial Flutter

RHYTHM: Regular or irregular
RATE: Fast (250-350 bpm) for Atrial, but ventricular rate is often slower
P-WAVE: Not observable, but saw-toothed flutter waves are present.
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES:



Atrial Fibrillation

RHYTHM:Irregular
RATE: Very Fast (>350 bpm) for Atrial, but ventricular rate may be slow, normal or fast.
P-WAVE: Absent- erratic waves are present
PR-INTERVAL: Absent
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec) But may be widened if there are conduction delays
NOTES:



Asystole

RHYTHM: Not present
RATE: Absent
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Absent
QRS: Absent
NOTES: Confirm with multiple leads & pulse ox or A-line (if the patient has one.)



First Degree Heart Block

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: prolonged(>0.20 sec)
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: A first degree AV block occurs when electrical impulses moving through the Atrioventricular (AV) node are delayed (but not blocked). First degree indicates slowed conduction without missed beats.



Second Degree
Heart Block Type 1

RHYTHM: Irregular but with progressively longer PR interval lengthening.
RATE: The underlying rate
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: progressively longer until a QRS complex is missed, then cycle repeats.
QRS: Normal (0.06-0.10 sec)
NOTES: Also known as Mobitz 1 or Wenckebach.



Second Degree
Heart Block Type 2

RHYTHM: Regular (atrial) & irregular (ventricular)
RATE: Characterized by Atrial rate usually faster than ventricular rate (usually slow).
P-WAVE: Normal form, but more P waves that QRS complexes.
PR-INTERVAL: Normal or prolonged
QRS: Normal or wide
NOTES: Also known as Mobitz 2



Third Degree Heart Block

RHYTHM: Regular, but atrial & ventricular rhythms are independent.
RATE: Characterized by Atrial rate usually normal & faster than ventricular rate
P-WAVE: Normal shape & size, may appear within QRS complexes.
PR-INTERVAL: Absent: the atria & ventricles beat independently.
NOTES: Complete heart block.



Ventricular Tachycardia
Monomorphic

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Fast (100-250 bpm)
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10 sec), bizarre appearance.
NOTES: Monomorphic VT is defined by three or more consecutive uniform pvc's.



Ventricular Tachycardia
Polymorphic

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: Fast (100-250 bpm)
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10 sec), bizarre appearance.
NOTES: Polymorphic VT is defined as an unstable rhythm with varying QRS complex morphology in any recorded ECG lead



Ventricular Tachycardia,
Torsade de Pointes

RHYTHM Irregular
RATE: Fast (200-250 bpm)
P-WAVE: Absent
PR-INTERVAL: Not measurable
QRS: Wide (>0.10 sec), bizarre appearance.
NOTES: Torsades is defined as the combination of polymorphic VT & a prolonged QT-interval.



Wolff-Parkinson-White Syndrome

RHYTHM: Regular unless atrial fibrillation present
RATE: Normal (60-100 bpm)
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Can be short (<0.12 sec)
QRS: Usually wide (> 0.12 sec)
NOTES: Look for short PR intervals and/or delta wave. A delta wave (positive or negative) distorts the early part of the QRS complex.



Pacemaker
Single Chamber Atrial

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: 60 bpm
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal
QRS: Normal
NOTES: When a patient is atrial paced, the spike comes before the P-wave.



Pacemaker
Single Chamber Ventricular

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: 60 bpm
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal
QRS: Wide, this is because the impulse in not originating from the SA- Node.
NOTES: When a patient is ventricularly paced, the spike comes before the QRS complex



Pacemaker
Atrial & Ventricular

RHYTHM: Regular
RATE: 60 bpm
P-WAVE: Normal
PR-INTERVAL: Normal
QRS: Wide, this is because the impulse in not originating from the SA- Node.
NOTES: When a patient is A/V paced there should be a spike in front of the P-wave, and another spike in front of the QRS complex. Also known as dual pacing.



Pacemaker, Failure To Pace

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE:
P-WAVE:
PR-INTERVAL:
QRS:
NOTES:Pacer spikes fail to appear. This malfunction can happen with A-pacing, V-pacing or A/V pacing



Pacemaker, Failure To Capture

RHYTHM: Irregular
RATE:
P-WAVE:
PR-INTERVAL:
QRS:
NOTES:Pacer spikes appear but are NOT followed by its associated p-wave or QRS complex. This malfunction can happen with A-pacing, V-pacing or A/V pacing



Pacemaker, undersensing

RHYTHM: Regular or Irregular
RATE:
P-WAVE:
PR-INTERVAL:
QRS:
NOTES: Pacermaker fails to sense intrinsic activty and sends the impulse to pace inappropriately.



Pacemaker, oversensing

RHYTHM: Regular or Irregular
RATE:
P-WAVE:
PR-INTERVAL:
QRS:
NOTES: Pacermaker inhibited due to sensing of P-waves & QRS complexes that do not exist. Pacemaker is too sensitive.
